How does the auction work?  

When the auction begins, our auctioneer will introduce the lot for sale and open the bidding at about one-half of its low estimated value. Once the first bid is placed, the auctioneer usually asks for higher bids in increments of 10 percent or less over the previous bid placed. For example, if the initial bid is for $1,000, the auctioneer would ask for new bids of $1,100; after that $1,200 and so on. The current bid is displayed prominently in all major currencies on a display board. What are the auction estimates? The pre-sale estimates are intended as a guide for prospective buyers. Any bid between the listed figures would, in our opinion, offer a fair chance of success. However, all lots, depending on the degree of competition, can realise prices either above or below the listed estimates.
